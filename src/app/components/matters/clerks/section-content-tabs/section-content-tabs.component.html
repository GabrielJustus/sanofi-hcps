PRODUCT RECOMMENDATION


30%
of drugstore clerks consider themselves empowered to recommend products to consumers


here we should question whether we see this public as an opportunity to make consumers switch from another product to ours: would this reduce the doctor's autonomy? Would this be good for us or does this public tend to recommend the cheapest products? This question will make us decide whether we want them to have more autonomy to make recommendations or not. From consumer surveys, we have learned that this public influences consumers from lower classes, who go to the doctor less frequently and go to independent drugstores more often.

56%
suggest a similar or generic product when the prescription contains only the name of the molecule and not the product name.

78%
